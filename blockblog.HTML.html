Cake Design

Cake decoration is one of the sugar arts that decoration elements to make plain cakes more visually interesting. Alternatively, cake can be molded and sculpted to resemble three dimenstional persons, places and things. Decorating a cake usually involves covering it with some form of icing and then using decorative sugar, candly, chocolate or icing decorations to embellish the cake.

Cake are decorated to marks a special celebration (such as a birthday or wedding). they can also marks national or religious holidays, or used to promote commercial enterprises. however, cakes may be baked and decoration for almost any social occasion.

The advent of temperature-controlled oven and the production of baking powder made baking cakes much easier. as temperature control technology improved, an increased emphasis on presentation and ornamentation developed.

Benefits of Cake Design

Cake began to take on decorative shapes, were adorned with additional icing formed into patterns and flowers, and food coloring was used to accent frosting or layers of cake. there is no official answers to who specifically began cake decorating.

Cakes have been decorated for many centurise for special occasion, Wedding cakes have the longest traditions and are considered the most artistic form, and christmas cakes being decorated began in the eighteen centuries, they were consider twelfth night cakes were only baked by the rich for the rich.

Types of Cakes

Fondant: also know as sugar paste or ready roll icing, exists in many different colors, and it is usually easy to handle once prepared properly. it must be rolled out with cornstarch to avoid sticking to any surface and to smooth it out, once completely smooth and thin enough, cake decorators are able to mold fondant into many different artistic expressions.

Royal icing: is a sweet white icing made by whipping fresh egg whites (or powdered egg whites, meringue powder) with icing sugar. Royal icing produces well-defined icing edges especially when decorating biscuits/ cookies and is ideal for piping intricate writing, borders,scrollwork and lacework on cakes. it dries very hard and preserves indenfinitely if stored in a cool, dry place, but is susceptible to soften and wilt in high humidity.

Gum paste: also know as florist pagste, is an edible, brittle material that dries quickly and can be scrulpted to make cake decorations such as flowers or molded designs.
